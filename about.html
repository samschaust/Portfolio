Accessibility and usability are the pillars by which I stand in my work. I aspire to create products and experiences that start by considering the needs of disabled users before applying those design lessons toward a general audience.

In my graduate coursework, I interviewed real users, built personas, developed prototypes, and tested my prototypes upon those same users. I hope to put these skills to practice in the next phase of my career.

My interest in user experience and user interface design (UX/UI) stems from my love of video games. The first moment I noticed the power of UI in game design was while playing horror-thriller Dead Space (2008). Since then, I have dreamed of creating diegetic UI and went so far as to attempt it in my M.S. thesis.

In my leisure time I enjoy to cook, spin records, take my dog Hiro on long walks, read Haruki Murakami novels, watch Criterion movies, and play through my enormous video game backlog.
