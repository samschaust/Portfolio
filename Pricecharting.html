Redesign of Pricecharting.com

Created during the four-month span of a Master's-level Directed Study course with two College of Design professors at the University of Minnesota. My advisors were Design Graduate Program Director Brad Hokanson and Professor Debra Lawton.


Pricecharting.com

UI

Over the course of the fall semester, I redesigned the desktop version of Pricecharting.com, a website used to monitor the prices of new and retro video games and systems. The website's audience consists of casual enthusiasts and dedicated collectors who use the Pricecharting database similar to how stock traders monitor stocks. The goal of the project was to modernize the website's appearance and search fuction â€” the most-used tool.

The project began with the creation of personas and user journey maps. I then interviewed real users, conducted usability tests on the existing website, and used their data in an affinity diagram exercise. For additional perspective, I conducted a competitor analysis.

With all of this data, I wireframed a new website. I turned those low-resolution wireframes into a high-resolution prototype in Adobe XD. The usability of that prototype was tested using Qualtrics. Additional refinements were made to the prototype before final turn-in.
